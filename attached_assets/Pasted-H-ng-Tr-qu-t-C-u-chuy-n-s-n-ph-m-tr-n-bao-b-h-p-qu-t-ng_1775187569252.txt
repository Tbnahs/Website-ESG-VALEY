Trên những triền núi cao hơn 1.000m, những người dân bản địa đã thay lời núi rừng, hái tay từng búp trà, từng trái quýt bằng tất cả lòng biết ơn và sự trân trọng để cùng viết nên một câu chuyện mới cho Trà Việt.

Trái quýt có vỏ dày, sần sùi với lớp lớp tinh dầu căng mọng ngấm sâu vào trong Trà, đẩy nhanh quá trình lên men của Trà chín. Khi để qua thời gian, Trà quýt tiếp tục lên men, đánh thức những tầng hương mật ngọt ngào nhất; chất trà và tinh dầu quýt chuyển hóa thành món quà tự nhiên cho sức khoẻ, phù hợp sử dụng hàng ngày mọi mùa trong năm.
Khi thưởng thức, bạn sẽ cảm nhận được vị ngọt thanh đặc trưng của quýt rừng len lỏi qua vị hồng Trà êm dịu, tròn trịa, để lại hậu vị ngọt sâu kéo dài như lời thủ thỉ của núi rừng Thái Nguyên.
Hồng Trà Quýt là lời cam kết của ESGValley trong việc tôn vinh các giá trị bản địa đặc sắc và phát triển bền vững. Thưởng thức một chén trà ấm là chạm vào tinh hoa của vùng đất Di sản, là lan tỏa niềm tự hào về bản sắc Việt Nam trường tồn.

HONG TRA QUYT - A SYMPHONY OF THE FOREST
Some encounters feel as if they were always meant to happen, deep within the great forests. If Diep Tra San is the pure breath of early morning, then Hong Tra Quyt unfolds like a rich and vibrant symphony. Ancient Shan Snow tea buds from Dong Phuc find their perfect harmony in the delicate, uplifting notes of wild citrus from Bach Thong. On mountain slopes rising over 1,000 meters, local people gather each tea bud and each citrus fruit by hand, with quiet gratitude and deep respect, as if speaking on behalf of the forest itself. Together, they shape a new story for Vietnamese tea.
Skilled artisans gently bring together ancient Shan tea buds and fragrant citrus peel. The tea is withered, lightly pan fired, sun dried, and fermented to the right point before being placed inside the whole citrus fruit and naturally dried again. The thick, textured peel, rich with essential oils, slowly infuses into the tea, encouraging a deeper fermentation. Over time, the tea continues to evolve, revealing layers of warm, honeyed aroma, as both tea and citrus transform into a natural gift for everyday wellbeing, suitable in any season.
With each sip, a gentle citrus sweetness weaves through the smooth, rounded body of the black tea, leaving behind a deep, lingering finish that feels like a quiet whisper from the forests of Thai Nguyen.
Hong Tra Quyt reflects ESGValley’s commitment to honoring local heritage and nurturing sustainable values. A warm cup is more than just tea, it is a touch of the land’s essence, and a way of sharing the enduring pride of Vietnamese identity.

Check the authenticity label and traceability code before use.
Use one whole citrus fruit per brew.
Warm your teaware beforehand (best enjoyed with a clay teapot and cups).
Use purified water at around 90 to 95°C.
Place the tea into the pot, pour in 150ml of water, and steep for 15 to 30 seconds to allow the citrus aroma and tea to fully harmonize. Pour all the tea into a serving pitcher, then into cups, and enjoy.
(Note: Pour out all the tea after each brew. Do not leave water sitting in the pot. Can be brewed up to 5 times.)

TRA QUYT ONG LAM GAC BEP – WHERE FOREST AROMAS LINGER, and Time Deepens the Taste
Some cultural values seem to have always belonged to the quiet rhythm of the Thai Nguyen highlands. If Hong Tra Quyt is a bright and vibrant harmony, then Tra Quyt Ong Lam Gac Bep is a deeper, more contemplative pause, where ancient Shan tea from Dong Phuc meets wild citrus, held within fresh bamboo and gently nurtured through seasons of slow kitchen smoke by the people of Dong Phuc.
Centuries old Shan Snow tea buds are carefully combined with finely cut wild citrus peel, a delicate gift from the forests of Bach Thong. Packed tightly into fresh green bamboo tubes, they begin a long journey over the kitchen fire, enduring days and months of patient tending.
Wrapped in the quiet veil of smoke, the fresh bamboo resin and wild citrus oils slowly seep into the tea, awakening the spirit of the ancient leaves. The tea is not only fermented, but also infused with smoke, creating a character all its own: a golden liquor like forest honey, a rich and full taste with a gentle astringency, and a deep, lingering sweetness.
To enjoy a cup of Tra Quyt Ong Lam Gac Bep is to taste the honeyed sweetness of wild citrus, the soft fragrance of bamboo, and the smooth depth of tea coming together in harmony. The finish lingers, deep and lasting, like a quiet whisper from the forests of Thai Nguyen carried through time, a precious gift for wellbeing.
More than just tea, Tra Quyt Ong Lam Gac Bep is ESGValley’s commitment to preserving and honoring rare, artisanal traditions. To hold a cup is to touch the enduring flow of Vietnamese culture, where tradition, fire, and nature come together to compose a timeless song for generations to come.

Thưởng thức Hồng Trà Shan là chạm vào tinh hoa ngàn năm, là cùng chúng tôi lan tỏa niềm tự hào về một biểu tượng văn hóa và bản sắc Việt Nam trường tồn. HONG TRA SHAN – THE DEEP INNER STRENGTH OF DONG PHUC HERITAGE
If Diep Tra San is the pure breath of early morning, then Hong Tra Shan carries a quiet depth, drawn from heritage tea trees in Dong Phuc, Thai Nguyen. In the gentle highland climate, ancient Shan tea buds, nourished by the richness of the earth, are ready to transform in a way that feels almost like art.
Continuing that journey of heritage, ESGValley and devoted artisans carefully handpick each Shan Snow bud by hand, treating them as nature’s own treasures. Through skilled craftsmanship and the traditional art of full fermentation, the soul of the tea is awakened into something deeper, fuller, and more expressive.
While most black teas are made from industrial tea grown in midland and lowland regions, Hong Tra Shan stands apart thanks to its origin from ancient Shan Snow trees in Dong Phuc. Naturally fully fermented, it carries a dense body, layered flavors, a rich infusion, and a long lingering finish, creating a character that is both bold and refined.
With each sip, the taste feels round and smooth, free from sharp astringency, replaced instead by a gentle sweetness that slowly spreads, like the soft glow of late afternoon sunlight over the mountains. More than just a refined drink for wellbeing, Hong Tra Shan reflects ESGValley’s commitment to preserving tradition while moving toward sustainability.
To drink Hong Tra Shan is to touch something timeless, to share in the pride of a cultural symbol deeply rooted in Vietnamese identity. Let each warm cup lead you into a landscape of drifting clouds and mountain winds, where the rich aroma of tea gently warms the soul and connects deeper human values.
